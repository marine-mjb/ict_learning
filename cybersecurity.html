Cybersecurity 101

Protect systems, networks, and data from digital threats

🛡️ What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. It's also known as information technology security. Every organization from banks to hospitals depends on strong cybersecurity.

# Core security principles - CIA Triad Confidentiality → Only authorized access Integrity → Data is accurate and unaltered Availability → Systems accessible when needed
💡 Pro tip: Always think "CIA" — Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability. These three principles form the foundation of every security strategy!
CIA Triad threat landscape risk management compliance
🎣 Common Threats

Phishing uses fake emails/websites to steal credentials. Malware includes viruses, ransomware, and spyware. Man-in-the-middle attacks intercept communications. Denial-of-service floods systems with traffic. Stay vigilant!

# Red flags in suspicious emails • Urgent language: "Your account will be closed!" • Generic greetings like "Dear User" • Mismatched URLs (hover before clicking) • Unexpected attachments or links
✅ Best practice: Never click links in unexpected emails. Type the website URL directly into your browser instead.
🔑 Strong Authentication

Use long, unique passwords (12+ characters) with mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Enable 2FA/MFA everywhere possible. Password managers help generate and store complex passwords securely.

# Password strength examples ❌ Weak: password123, qwerty, 123456 ✅ Strong: C8!mP$9#vLq@2wR ✅ Better: Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple (passphrase) # Always enable 2FA when available Something you KNOW (password) + Something you HAVE (phone)
🚀 2FA tip: Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy) instead of SMS when possible — they're more secure!
🔒 Encryption & HTTPS

Encryption scrambles data so only authorized parties can read it. HTTPS (HTTP Secure) encrypts communication between your browser and websites. Look for the padlock icon in your address bar!

# How HTTPS protects you Browser ←→ [ENCRYPTED TUNNEL] ←→ Website Data stays private even if intercepted Padlock ✅ = secure connection # Always check before entering passwords https://yourbank.com ✅ (secure) http://yourbank.com ❌ (unencrypted)
⚡ Security habit: Never enter sensitive information on websites without HTTPS. Install browser extensions like HTTPS Everywhere for extra protection.
